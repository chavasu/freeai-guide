FAQ
Common questions answered
The questions we get asked most about Claude vs ChatGPT.
Is Claude smarter than ChatGPT in 2026?
Neither is definitively smarter. Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.4 score within 1–2% of each other on most benchmarks. Claude leads on coding (SWE-bench: 80.8% vs ~80%) and writing quality. ChatGPT leads slightly on GPQA reasoning (92.8% vs 91.3%) and dominates multimodal tasks. Anyone claiming one is clearly "smarter" is either selling something or hasn't tested both on real tasks.
Can Claude generate images like ChatGPT can?
No. As of April 2026, Claude cannot generate images. It can analyze and describe images you upload, but it does not create them. ChatGPT Plus includes DALL-E 3 for image generation and Sora for short video clips. If image generation is part of your workflow, ChatGPT is the clear choice.
Which has a better free tier — Claude or ChatGPT?
They're different rather than one being better. Claude's free tier gives you a larger context window (200K vs 128K) and is better for long documents and coding. ChatGPT's free tier gives you image generation and better web search. For most casual users, ChatGPT's free tier offers more features. For professionals analyzing large documents, Claude's free tier is more capable.
Why do developers prefer Claude?
According to developer surveys in 2025–2026, roughly 70% of developers who use AI coding tools prefer Claude for complex tasks. Key reasons: Claude Code (a terminal-based autonomous agent included with Pro), consistently high SWE-bench scores, and the fact that most popular AI coding IDEs like Cursor and Windsurf use Claude as their primary model by default. The 200K context window also allows Claude to hold an entire large codebase in memory.
Is it worth paying $20/month for either?
For regular users (daily use, work tasks), yes — either paid plan is worth it. The free tiers hit daily limits quickly for heavy users. At $20/month for Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus, you get 5× the usage, priority access, and unlocked features. The better question is whether $20/month is worth it vs using multiple free tiers (Claude free + ChatGPT free + Gemini free) strategically. For many users, combining free tiers covers most needs.
Which is safer for business data?
Both offer enhanced data protection on paid business/enterprise plans. Neither free tier should be used for confidential business data. Claude's Constitutional AI approach gives it a slightly lower hallucination rate and more conservative behavior with sensitive content. For regulated industries (healthcare, legal, finance) requiring HIPAA or SOC 2 compliance, both offer enterprise tiers — verify current compliance certifications directly with each company before committing.
What's the context window difference?
Claude has a larger default context window: 200K tokens on the standard paid plan vs ChatGPT's 128K. In practice, 200K tokens can hold approximately 150,000 words — enough for a full novel or a large codebase. Both offer 1M token context at their maximum API tier (beta). For most everyday tasks, the difference doesn't matter. For processing very long documents, entire books, or large codebases, Claude's 200K default is a real advantage.
