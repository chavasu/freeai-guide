What AI tools must have

Before you recommend or buy any AI tool, check these core requirements.

  • Clear core use case: know whether you need chat, writing, image, video, coding, automation, or enterprise AI.
  • Reliable output quality: test the tool on 3 to 5 real tasks before committing.
  • Transparent pricing: check monthly limits, credit systems, seat pricing, and overage costs.
  • Export and portability: make sure you can export outputs, data, or projects easily.
  • Team fit: confirm collaboration, roles, approvals, and workspace options if others will use it.
  • Integrations: look for tools that connect to your existing stack like Google Workspace, WordPress, Slack, CRM, or cloud storage.
  • Speed and usability: the best tool is often the one your team will actually use every day.
  • Commercial rights: verify usage rights for content, images, voices, and generated assets.
  • Privacy and security: review data retention, training policies, and admin controls.
  • Affiliate opportunity: if you are publishing reviews, prioritize tools with recurring commissions or strong SMB purchase intent.
