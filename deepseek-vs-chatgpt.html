The Origin Story
How a Chinese startup rattled the entire AI industry
Understanding where DeepSeek came from explains why this comparison matters so much.

In January 2025, DeepSeek released its R1 reasoning model and immediately became the most downloaded app on the US App Store. The AI world went into shock — not because R1 was marginally better than existing models, but because of how it was built. DeepSeek's entire training cost was approximately $5.5 million, using 2,048 Nvidia H800 GPUs over 55 days. OpenAI reportedly spent over $100 million training comparable models. Nvidia's stock dropped 17% in a single session as investors realised that the hardware arms race they'd been funding might not be the only path to frontier AI.

DeepSeek achieves this efficiency through a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture — while the model has 671 billion total parameters, only 37 billion are active for any given query. Instead of running the full model for every request (as ChatGPT's dense transformer does), DeepSeek routes each query to the most relevant specialist sub-networks. The result is frontier performance at a fraction of the compute cost. By April 2026, DeepSeek has grown to approximately 125 million monthly active users, 5.7 billion API calls per month, and 170,000+ GitHub stars — making it the most popular open-source AI project in the world.

🏭
Architecture note: ChatGPT's GPT-5.4 uses a dense transformer that activates all 1.8 trillion parameters on every query — excellent for versatility and breadth, but computationally expensive. DeepSeek's MoE approach activates only the parameters needed for each specific task. This is why DeepSeek can offer comparable performance at dramatically lower cost.

Privacy & Security
The most important section — read this before deciding
DeepSeek's privacy situation is unique among major AI tools. It deserves a thorough, honest explanation.
⚠️ DeepSeek Cloud Privacy Warning

DeepSeek's hosted chatbot and API store all conversation data on servers located in the People's Republic of China. Under Chinese law (specifically the 2017 National Intelligence Law and 2021 Data Security Law), Chinese companies are legally required to cooperate with government intelligence requests. Unlike in the US, EU, or UK, there is no independent judiciary DeepSeek can appeal to in order to challenge such requests.

This is not theoretical risk. Multiple governments and regulatory bodies have taken action:

🇮🇹 Italy — banned from app stores 🇰🇷 South Korea — government ban 🇦🇺 Australia — federal agency ban 🇺🇸 US Navy — prohibited 🇬🇧 UK — government review 🇮🇳 India — app stores review 🇹🇼 Taiwan — government ban

What this means practically: Do not use the DeepSeek cloud app or API for client data, medical records, legal documents, financial information, proprietary business data, or anything covered by HIPAA, GDPR, SOC 2, or similar regulations. For casual, non-sensitive use — code you'd share publicly, math problems, general learning — the risk is relatively low for most individuals.

🔒 The privacy solution: self-hosting. DeepSeek's open-source weights solve the privacy problem entirely. Running DeepSeek locally means your data never leaves your servers. Tools like Ollama and LM Studio make this achievable without deep infrastructure expertise — though you do need a capable GPU (RTX 4090 for the full model).

FAQ
The questions people actually search for
Honest, direct answers to the most common DeepSeek vs ChatGPT questions.
Is DeepSeek better than ChatGPT?
It depends on the task. For maths, coding (Python accuracy), and cost — DeepSeek is equal to or better than ChatGPT at a fraction of the price. For writing quality, image generation, voice, web browsing, and privacy — ChatGPT is clearly better. DeepSeek is not a full replacement for ChatGPT; it's a specialist tool that excels in specific technical areas while charging nothing.
Is DeepSeek safe to use?
For non-sensitive personal use (learning, coding hobby projects, general questions) — the risk is relatively low for most individuals. For professional, business, client, medical, legal, or financial data — no, the cloud version is not safe. All cloud conversations are stored on servers in China and subject to Chinese law. Multiple countries and institutions have banned it for official use. The self-hosted open-source version is safe — your data never leaves your hardware.
Is DeepSeek really free?
Yes, completely free for consumers. DeepSeek's web app and mobile app provide unlimited access to DeepSeek V4, including the DeepThink chain-of-thought reasoning mode, at no cost. There are no daily message caps, no paywalled features, and no subscription required. The API has a small per-token cost ($0.27/1M input tokens) but the consumer product is genuinely unlimited and free.
Can I use DeepSeek for my business?
It depends on your industry and data type. For non-sensitive internal workflows, general research, and code generation — potentially yes. For anything involving client data, regulated information, or any EU, US healthcare, or finance compliance requirements — no, the cloud API is not suitable. Your best option for business use that wants DeepSeek's economics is self-hosting the open-source weights on your own infrastructure, which eliminates the China data concern entirely.
Why is DeepSeek so cheap?
Two main reasons. First, DeepSeek uses a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture that activates only 37 billion of its 671 billion parameters for each query — dramatically reducing compute cost per request. Second, DeepSeek trained its entire model for approximately $5.5 million — compared to OpenAI's reported $100M+ for comparable models. Lower training and inference costs allow DeepSeek to charge less while still running a viable business, partly subsidised by the Chinese government's strategic interest in building domestic AI capability.
How do I run DeepSeek locally (without sending data to China)?
The easiest method: install Ollama (ollama.com), then run "ollama pull deepseek-r1:14b" in your terminal. This downloads the 14B parameter distilled model (requires 16GB VRAM) and lets you chat with it entirely locally. For a GUI, install LM Studio (lmstudio.ai) — it provides a ChatGPT-like interface running locally. The 7B model runs on most modern gaming GPUs (8GB VRAM). All data stays entirely on your machine.
Is DeepSeek's chain-of-thought reasoning better than ChatGPT's?
DeepSeek shows its reasoning process transparently on every query for free. ChatGPT's reasoning mode (o1) is available only on paid plans. For educational purposes — understanding how an AI arrives at an answer — DeepSeek's visible chain-of-thought is genuinely valuable and not matched by ChatGPT on the free tier. The quality of the reasoning itself is broadly comparable for maths and code, though ChatGPT's o1 model edges ahead on complex abstract reasoning.
Does DeepSeek have image generation?
No. As of April 2026, DeepSeek is entirely text and code focused. It cannot generate images, voice, or video. If image generation is part of your workflow, ChatGPT (DALL-E 3) or Gemini (Imagen) are the right tools. DeepSeek can analyse images you upload, but cannot create them.
